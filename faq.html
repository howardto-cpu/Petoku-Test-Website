When you sprinkle Petoku onto your dog's food, saliva becomes the delivery system. As your dog chews and swallows, the active ingredients are distributed throughout the mouth and carried across teeth, gums, and oral surfaces.

The key is Ascophyllum nodosum (brown algae), our hero ingredient. It works both topically and systemically: after digestion, it is secreted back into saliva, where it prevents plaque-forming bacteria from adhering to tooth surfaces. Meanwhile, the Glucose Oxidase enzyme reacts with glucose in the mouth to produce a natural low-level antibacterial effect continuously during meals. Zinc Citrate helps inhibit bacterial adhesion and supports healthy gums, contributing to a cleaner oral environment over time. The result is a multi-front approach that tackles plaque, tartar, and breath at the same time.
Most dogs show fresher breath within 1 to 2 weeks. Visible plaque and tartar reduction typically occurs within 3 to 8 weeks of consistent daily use. Results vary based on your dog's existing dental condition, size, and diet. Dogs with more significant buildup may take longer to show visible change, while those starting earlier often see results sooner. Consistency every day is what drives results.

Petoku is safe for most adult dogs, but please review the following cautions carefully:

Puppies under 1 year old: Wait until your dog is at least 12 months before introducing Petoku, as their systems are still developing.

Pregnant or nursing dogs: Out of caution, we advise against use during pregnancy or while nursing without consulting your vet first.

Dogs with liver disease: Several ingredients in the formula are metabolized through the liver. Use only under veterinary supervision if your dog has a known liver condition.

Dogs with thyroid disease: Our formula contains Ascophyllum nodosum, a brown algae that naturally contains iodine. Excess iodine intake can interfere with thyroid function in dogs with pre-existing thyroid conditions. Consult your vet before use.

Dogs with kidney disease: Some ingredients are processed through the kidneys, and Nutritional Yeast contains phosphorus which may be a concern for dogs on a phosphorus-restricted diet. Check with your vet if your dog has a known kidney condition.

Dogs with a known chicken allergy: The formula contains Hydrolyzed Chicken Liver. While hydrolyzed proteins are significantly less allergenic than intact proteins, dogs with a severe chicken protein allergy should avoid use or consult a veterinarian first.

Dogs on anticoagulant medications: Green Tea Extract contains vitamin K and certain polyphenols that can interact with anticoagulant drugs like warfarin. If your dog is on any blood-thinning medication, consult your vet before use.

When in doubt, a quick check with your vet is always the right call.
